My Story



Born in 2001 in Mysore, a heritage city in Karnataka. Raised by doctors, I was a keen and curious child. Without much interest in sports, and with parents often busy, I spent most of my time learning either at school or playing with my toys alone in my room. Being an only child was sometimes a lonely experience.

As I grew up, I made many friends in my apartment complex and at school, and I consistently excelled in my exams until the 10th grade. Then, life took a turn. My parents wanted me to become a doctor, and I agreed because other professions were practically off-limits at home. I was good at biology, so it didn't feel like much of a debate. However, during my 12th grade, I found myself losing interest, perhaps due to a lack of motivation. Eventually, my marks — and fate — decided that medicine was not my path. Although I was heartbroken then, I am now grateful because it led me to discover the incredible world of engineering.

Engineering taught me not only how to fix bugs in code but also how to solve real-life problems. As I made progress in my studies and extracurricular activities, I learned more about myself — my love for problem-solving and my desire to help others. This passion kept growing and shaping me.

After completing my B.Tech, I decided to pursue my master's degree at IIIT-H. Before taking that step, I wanted to experience life beyond the comfort of my home. Driven by a desire to step into the real world and experience hostel life, I took an internship at IIIT-H and worked with a startup. There, I witnessed how challenging and exciting the world could be when you’re on your own. Fortunately, with the support of my family, faith, and well-wishers, I navigated the tough times and emerged stronger.

Now, as a master's student at IIIT-H, I'm focused on becoming a Product Manager, ready to channel my passion for problem-solving into creating impactful solutions.
